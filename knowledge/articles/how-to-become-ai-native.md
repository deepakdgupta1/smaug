A comprehensive framework for understanding the difference between AI-augmented and AI-native development practices, and why the gap is accelerating.

## Key Takeaways

- **Two Tiers of AI Adoption**: Being "AI-augmented" (using AI as a search engine) versus "AI-native" (using AI as a thought partner for iteration) produces fundamentally different outcomes
- **Measurable Performance Gap**: Anthropic's reports show early adopters get "significantly more value" not from using AI more often, but from using it differently
- **Wage Premium Evidence**: Workers with AI skills command a 56% wage premium (PwC); 39% of core skills will change by 2030 (WEF)
- **Skills > Tools**: Becoming AI-native is not about learning more tools, but about changing how you think about problems and iterating with AI as a partner
- **Judgment, Not Prompts**: The real differentiator is not sophisticated prompting but informed decision-making in the iteration loop
