## Summary

A framework for making AI coding-agent harness evolution observable and principled. Most harnesses are tuned by hand or rely on brittle trial-and-error self-evolution. This work introduces three layers: components as revertible files, experience as condensed evidence from millions of trajectory tokens, and decisions as falsifiable predictions checked against task outcomes. Enables systematic harness improvement based on actual execution evidence.

## Key Takeaways

- Most coding-agent harnesses lack principled evolution methods
- Three-layer framework: revertible components, trajectory evidence, falsifiable decisions
- Harness evolution can be made observable and measurable
- Trajectory tokens from millions of runs provide empirical evidence for harness choices
- Predictions can be validated against actual task outcomes for feedback loops
