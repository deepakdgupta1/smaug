## Summary

The CODEX Multi Agent Playbook Level 1 is a comprehensive guide to understanding and implementing swarms in multi-agent systems. Following Parts 1 and 2 which covered subagents fundamentals and custom agent roles, this section focuses on coordinating multiple agents executing in parallel, each with its own clear scope, working toward shared goals.

The playbook emphasizes that effective swarms require three critical elements: proper planning, orchestration strategy, and high-quality context. It distinguishes between treating subagents as a novelty versus understanding them as a workforce, and warns that throwing agents at problems without strategy leads to conflicts, wasted tokens, and poor results.

## Key Takeaways

- **Swarms as Workforce**: Swarms are the evolution of thinking about subagents as a novelty into viewing them as an organized workforce
- **Parallel Execution**: Multiple agents working in parallel with clear scopes and goals is the foundation of effective swarms
- **Three Success Factors**: Planning, orchestration, and context quality determine whether a swarm works or fails
- **Strategy Matters**: Lack of strategy leads to conflicts, wasted tokens, and poor output quality
- **Custom Agent Roles**: The ability to define agent roles, assign models, and write orchestration prompts is essential
- **Build on Fundamentals**: Effective swarms require understanding of subagents basics, orchestrators vs workers, and agent role definition
