## Summary

A foundational course on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and its application to building production-grade AI agents. The article explains that most AI agents fail because they lack access to organization-specific data. RAG solves this by giving AI models access to internal documentation, databases, and knowledge bases, transforming them from generalists into domain experts. By the end of the course, users can build AI agents that answer questions using proprietary data with accuracy and citations.

## Key Takeaways

- Core problem: AI models don't know your company's data, products, documentation, or customer history
- RAG transforms AI from "well-read stranger" to "colleague who read every company document"
- Implementation process: convert questions to embeddings, search document stores, augment prompts with retrieved context
- Use cases: enterprise Q&A, customer support, internal knowledge access, compliance-aware responses
- RAG is essential infrastructure for moving AI from demos to production business applications
