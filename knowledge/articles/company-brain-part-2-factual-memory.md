## Summary

Ashwin Gopinath continues his Company Brain series by diving into factual memory - the foundational layer of organizational intelligence. Factual memory represents a company's ability to answer fundamental questions about what exists and what happened across all work artifacts: documents, tickets, CRM notes, code, dashboards, emails, and more.

The key insight is that factual memory is not a central repository or wiki - it must be embedded in the natural workflows where people already work. Companies fail at this because they try to force people out of their natural tools (Slack, docs, meetings, email) into dedicated knowledge bases. Real factual memory must be personalized, permissioned, connected across tools, and accessible in the moment work happens.

## Key Takeaways

- **Not a Repository**: Factual memory cannot be built as a centralized knowledge base - people don't work in repositories, they work in docs, Slack, meetings, tickets, CRM systems, spreadsheets, GitHub, and email
- **Core Questions**: Real factual memory answers: what is this, what happened, where is the source, who owns it, when did it change, how does this thing work?
- **Universal Coverage**: Must preserve facts across all places where work leaves a trace
- **Distributed Access**: Success requires bringing intelligence to where work happens, not moving work to where knowledge lives
