# The Ultimate Hermes Guide - Building a 4-Profile Team That Survives Past Day 30

Guide for scaling from a single Hermes agent to a coherent 4-profile team that maintains distinct roles and doesn't collapse into a single blurry agent after a month. The critical layer most operators miss: the operator layer that keeps distributed teams aligned.

Based on real-world experience running researcher, writer, coder, and orchestrator roles on a single claude-sonnet-4.6 profile until everything blurred together—and the patterns that prevent that collapse.

## Key Takeaways

### The Mental Model Shift

- **Wrong**: One genius AI that does everything
- **Better**: Small team with distinct roles, clear handoffs, less context pollution

### The 4-Role Team

- **Orchestrator**: Coordination and routing
- **Alan**: (Role TBD from context)
- **Mira**: (Role TBD from context)  
- **Turing**: (Role TBD from context)

### The Operator Layer (Day 2+)

What separates teams that work on day 1 from those that work on day 30:

1. **Handoff Contracts**: Explicit interfaces between roles
2. **Memory-KPI per Profile**: Metrics for what each agent should remember
3. **Policy Gates per Role**: Rules about when to hand off vs. handle internally
4. **Day-30 Failure Modes**: Patterns to watch for and prevent
