A practical guide to finding use cases for AI agents. After installing OpenClaw and staring at it without knowing what to do, the author developed a philosophy: treat AI as an assistant to guide you in the right direction, not to replace thinking. The approach emphasizes understanding tasks before automating them—only automate what you already know how to do manually.

The key insight is moving beyond vague answers like "anything" or "everything" to specific, purposeful applications. This requires defining the actual grunt work that can be delegated while you focus on verification and decision-making.

## Key Takeaways
- AI agents work best when you assign them specific grunt work, then verify results
- Don't automate things you don't understand how to do manually
- Finding agent use cases requires defining concrete tasks with clear scope
- Personal philosophy: AI assists your thinking rather than replacing it
- Multi-agent setups take time to configure and optimize properly
