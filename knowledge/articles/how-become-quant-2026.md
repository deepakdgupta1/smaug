## Summary

A comprehensive learning path for becoming a quantitative trader, written from the perspective of someone with experience in the field. The article outlines what entry-level quants earn ($300K-$500K total comp at top firms in 2025) and provides a layered, sequential curriculum that takes approximately 18 months of dedicated work.

## Key Takeaways

- **Quant trading is about math, not stock picking** - Focus on statistical relationships, pricing inefficiencies, and structural edges from human error
- **Probability is foundational** - Everything in quantitative finance reduces to understanding odds and whether the odds are in your favor
- **Layered learning path** - Each concept builds on the last; you cannot skip levels
- **Real work required** - 18 months of actual problem-solving work (not video tutorials) can take someone from zero knowledge to competence
- **AI/ML hiring in finance grew 88% YoY** - Strong demand for quants with technical skills
- **Conditional thinking** - Understanding conditional probability and complex probability concepts is essential

## Learning Foundation

1. **Probability & Statistics** - The language of uncertainty
2. **Pricing Models** - Understanding market mechanics
3. **Risk Management** - Protecting capital
4. **Implementation** - From theory to real trading systems
