## Summary

A reflection on starting Abundance, a new venture focused on building an AI-driven capital allocation system. The article argues that AI fundamentally changes the capital allocation problem by enabling agents to process more information, connect more dots, and evaluate possibilities with consistent standards beyond human capability.

Capital allocation is one of the most consequential economic decisions—it determines which drugs get developed, which technologies get built, which ideas survive. Currently it relies on fragile human judgment, but AI agents can optimize this system to turn scarce resources into creation more effectively.

The company is starting in public markets (fastest feedback cycle) and plans to extend across other asset classes over time, keeping operations private rather than licensing the technology.

## Key Takeaways

- **Information Processing**: AI agents can absorb and connect more information than individual human investors
- **Consistency**: Agents provide consistent evaluation standards across thousands of opportunities
- **Optimization**: What was left to individual judgment becomes an optimizable system
- **Macro Impact**: Better capital allocation doesn't just improve investing—it determines what gets built and created in the economy
- **Speed**: Feedback loop in public markets validates hypothesis quickly
