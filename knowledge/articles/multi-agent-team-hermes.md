## Summary
A guide to building specialized multi-agent teams in Hermes instead of forcing a single generalist AI to handle all roles. The article emphasizes that Hermes profiles are not just cosmetic personas but isolated agent environments that enable role-based agents with cleaner boundaries and more durable specialization. The key insight is that moving from one agent to a real team starts with isolation, not better prompting.

## Key Takeaways
- **Wrong Mental Model**: "I need one genius AI that does everything" leads to blurred personalities, messy context, and noisy memory.
- **Right Mental Model**: "I need a small team with distinct roles, clear handoffs, and less context pollution."
- **Hermes Profiles as Isolated Environments**: Profiles separate memory, sessions, skills, personality, configuration, cron state, and gateway behavior—creating genuine agents, not just renamed personas.
- **Role-Based Specialization**: One profile can be a strategist, another research-first, another optimized for communication, another focused on implementation. Each becomes more coherent in isolation.
- **Isolation First**: The leap from one agent to a real multi-agent team starts with isolation, not with better prompting. Less context pollution enables more durable specialization.
