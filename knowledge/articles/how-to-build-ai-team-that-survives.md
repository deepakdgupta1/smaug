A critical guide to building AI teams that survive in production. The article identifies why 90% of "AI teams" fail within 30 days: not because agents are dumb, but because nobody's watching them. The core thesis is that real AI teams require three foundational rules: every agent must have a specific job description (not a vibe), real-time visibility into agent behavior and outputs, and reliable hosting infrastructure (not a laptop).

The piece outlines what functional AI teams look like in 2026, including specialized agent roles like content writers that pull trending topics and schedule posts, outreach SDRs that research and personalize cold emails, and customer support bots that handle inquiries. It emphasizes the infrastructure gap: building agents locally is easy, but shipping them at scale requires persistent monitoring, proper state management, and handling edge cases like broken APIs.

## Key Takeaways
- 90% of indie AI projects fail not from bad agents, but from lack of infrastructure and monitoring
- Every agent needs a narrow, repeatable job description and measurable success criteria
- Real-time observability is non-negotiable—silent failures are the deadliest
- Hosting on personal machines guarantees failure; production agents need reliable infrastructure
- The infrastructure layer is what separates toy projects from production AI teams
