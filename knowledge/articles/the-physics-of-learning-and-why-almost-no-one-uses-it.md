# The Physics of Learning (And Why Almost No One Uses It)

Science has identified replicable learning mechanics that work like laws of physics, yet education systems still ignore them.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Active Learning > Passive Consumption** - Actively solving problems produces more learning than passively watching videos, lectures, or re-reading notes

2. **The Forgetting Curve** - Information decay is mathematically modelable and predictable; review prevents forgetting

3. **Obvious ≠ Implemented** - We know what works (active learning, spaced repetition, problem-solving focus) but most education systems still rely on pure lecture formats with minimal review

4. **Initial Explanation + Maximum Practice Time** - The optimal structure is minimum effective dose of explanation followed by vast majority of time spent actively solving problems

The paradox: Educational research has established clear, scientifically-validated best practices, but institutional inertia prevents widespread adoption. These aren't theoretical—they're reproducible, tested repeatedly, and as fundamental as physics.
