## Summary

An analysis of AI adoption failure in enterprise settings based on conversations with 100+ C-Suite executives. Companies are spending millions on AI yet seeing minimal day-to-day impact. The piece argues that models are good enough—the problem is implementation and integration with existing business systems. Explores why agent frameworks that preserve systems of record succeed where big migrations fail.

## Key Takeaways

- Models (GPT-5.5, Opus 4.7, etc.) are production-ready and pricing has plummeted; they're not the blocker
- Enterprise AI failure is not a model problem but an integration problem
- Agents that work within existing systems (AP, CRM, finance) succeed; those requiring migration fail
- 12+ month deployment timelines and 30% data decay rates indicate organizational, not technical, constraints
- The next wave of AI ROI depends on preserving existing infrastructure while adding agent capabilities
