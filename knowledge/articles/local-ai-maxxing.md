The author rebuilt their Hermes agent to focus exclusively on local AI research. After 3 months of split attention (DeFi and AI research 50/50), they realized their DeFi research was good but the writing outputs were poor—requiring 50-80% manual rewriting. They deleted 17 skills, archived 3 months of research, and pivoted the entire system to a single mission: learn everything about running AI locally.

Running on a Mac Mini M4 (16GB unified memory), the agent (Oz) now researches local inference, new models, inference frameworks, benchmarks, and agent tooling. The key insight: agents are excellent researchers but poor writers. They produce raw material that requires significant human refinement.

For serious AI development in 2026, local-first setups are becoming critical infrastructure rather than an experimental side project. Running models locally provides control, cost efficiency, and independence from vendor constraints.

## Key Takeaways
- AI agents excel at research, not at polished writing
- Research output requires 50-80% human rewriting before publication
- Local AI inference is becoming essential infrastructure
- Mac Mini M4 with 16GB is practical for 24/7 agent operation
- Hermes agents can focus deeply when given a single mission
