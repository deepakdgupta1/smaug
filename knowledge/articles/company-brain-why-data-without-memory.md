## Summary

Ashwin Gopinath explores the organizational coordination problem: most companies accumulate data but lack real institutional memory. This creates friction where conversations lose context, meetings generate ambiguous follow-ups, and different people leave with different understandings of decisions.

Drawing on Paul Graham's "founder mode" concept - maintaining direct contact with company truth (customer pain, product tradeoffs, unresolved commitments, weak signals) - Gopinath argues that AI's ability to accelerate work speed has exposed the fragility of shared context. The real problem isn't about technology first; it's about human coordination at scale. His startup Sentra was built to solve this: can an organization remember enough to reason and act coherently?

## Key Takeaways

- **Coordination is Hard**: The core problem is human coordination during growth, not fundamentally an agent problem
- **Data ≠ Memory**: Having lots of data doesn't mean having institutional memory or a coherent context graph
- **Founder Mode**: Staying in contact with company truth requires understanding customer pain, tradeoffs, commitments, and weak signals before they become metrics
- **Fragile Context**: AI's speed amplifies the problem by moving faster than shared understanding can keep pace
- **Missing Layer**: Few companies have real memory or context graphs, and fewer still preserve facts in permissioned, personalized, connected ways
