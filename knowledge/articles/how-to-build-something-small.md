## Summary

This article challenges the conventional wisdom that bigger is always better. Zara Zhang argues that learning to build small, focused products is more valuable than chasing scale. She shares three projects she built as side projects (Frontend Slides, Follow Builders, Tab Out) and explains why positioning and narrow focus matter more than trying to serve everyone.

## Key Takeaways

- **Positioning is Everything**: Most products fail because they target too wide. Being everything to everybody means being nothing to nobody.
- **Build for Fun, Not Just Profit**: In an era of abundant software, building can be a hobby and learning exercise rather than purely utilitarian.
- **Small Things First**: Every big thing starts as a small thing. Starting small allows you to test positioning and market fit before scaling.
