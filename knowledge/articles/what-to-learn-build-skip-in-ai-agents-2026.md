Strategic guidance on what skills, projects, and technologies matter in AI agents in 2026. Written by someone with 2+ years building in the space, multiple $250k+ offers, and current technical leadership at a stealth company.

## Summary

The AI agent landscape changes monthly. Frameworks become legacy in quarters. Benchmarks get gamed and replaced. The conventional path of sequential learning and job progression no longer applies—experts with the right taste can ship production work in sprints.

The article distinguishes signal from noise: durable primitives matter more than this week's framework API surface. Expertise compounds from debugging real systems and making boring choices—not from chasing the latest launch.

## Key Takeaways

- Every roadmap is obsolete within a month; frameworks and benchmarks become legacy quickly
- Conventional expertise (debugging at 2am, watching systems break) still compounds
- What stopped compounding: knowing this week's framework API
- The winning strategy: pick durable primitives early, ignore the rest
- Six months from now, frameworks will differ; primitive understanding won't
- AI removed the 2-year experience requirement for shipping production work
